Three types of crimes against individuals include:
  • Industrial Esponiage

  • Money Laundering

  • Extortion



Industrial Esponiage

Definition of industrial esponiage according to Collins Dictionary:
attempting to obtain trade secrets by dishonest means, as by telephone- or computer-tapping, infiltration of a competitor's workforce.

Examples of industrial esponiage crimes in the past with the absence of technology
In 2001 rival consumer good corporations used industrial esponiage. Proctor and Gamble workers went undercover as dumpster
divers and went through Unilever's trash in search of intel that might enable the company to gain an edge over their rival.

How have technology evolved the way industrial esponiage is committed?
Today most of the interesting data is stored on computer systems. One of the most successful technologies in unauthorized deploying and
installing software on systems are worms, viruses and other malware by email spam, internet spam or any other means (Schulz, 2006).

What are actions/responses that may mitigate the effect of technology on industrial esponiage ?
To mitigate the effect of this companies are encouraged to do checks on the system as often as possible for any worms, viruses or any other problems on their computer systems. An antivirus system is used as a protection against this and some can even be used to trace the origin of virus ("Learn about malware and how to protect all your devices against it," n.d).

How can hacking be mitigated with the use of technology?
Hacking is type of industrial espionage that can be mitigated by installing protections, updating firewalls and updating virus databases.


Money Laundering

Definition of Money laundering according to Collins Dictionary:
Money laundering is the crime of processing stolen money through a legitimate business or sending it abroad to a foreign bank, to hide the fact that
the money was illegally obtained.

Examples of money laundering crimes in the past with the absence of technology
Money laundering is first seen with individuals hiding wealth from the state to avoid taxation or confiscation or a combination of both
(Money Laundering - Then and Now, 2016).

How have technology evolved the way money laundering is committed?
Legitimate websites are being used as payment processors to launder vast amounts of money. Drugs can be ordered online and the transaction will appear as something harmless on your statement, such as a flowers purchase. Last year it was alleged that an ISIS operative in the US had used eBay to 'sell' computer printers and received payments for these transactions from overseas via PayPal (Bell, 2018).

What are actions/responses that may mitigate the effect of technology on money laundering ?
New innovations in technology are being created to monitor transactions and are being used to help identify suspicious behaviour and patterns amongst legitimate payments and interactions (Bell, 2018).

How can embezzlement be mitigated with the use of technology?
Embezzlement which is a type of money laundering can be mitigated using accounting packages, like operating systems, which are continously being
patched due to newly discovered vulnerabilities which are used by employees to commit fraud (Hammarberg, 2015).


Extortion

Definition of extortion according to Collins Dictionary:
Extortion is the crime of obtaining something from someone, especially money, by using force or threats.

Examples of extortion crimes in the past with the absence of technology
Before the emergence of technology, extortion was done using industrial espionage to get information about a business or company and forcing these
business to give criminals money to prevent sensitive information would from being released.

How have technology evolved the way extortion is committed?
In a common form of cyber extortion, the victim receives an email saying that their information will be released to the public, their family, and their friends if a ransom is not paid. Criminals may say that they have placed software on the victim's computer that allowed them to steal documents, photos, passwords, or other sensitive information.

What are actions/responses that may mitigate the effect of technology on extortion?
Don't give out sensitive information such as passwords to anyone and be careful of what you download and the websites yo go on because these might have
spyware.

How can extortion be mitigated with the use of technology?
Extortion can be mitigated using technology when a software distributor announces updates for programs and software, users should install them as soon as
possible. Most times softwares are updated because there might be vulnerability in current versions.
